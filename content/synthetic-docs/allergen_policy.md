# Allergen Policy

## Purpose

Nura Wellness sells fictional supplement products through a direct-to-consumer storefront. The support team must answer allergen questions using approved product labels, ingredient records, and this policy. If a customer asks about a serious allergy, support should avoid personal safety judgments and recommend reviewing the product label with a qualified professional.

Allergen information must be handled carefully because customers may depend on it when deciding whether to purchase or use a product. Support agents should quote approved allergen statements and avoid guessing based on ingredient names alone.

## Major Allergen Statement

Nura products are made in a shared facility that also processes milk, soy, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, sesame, fish, and shellfish ingredients. Not every product contains those allergens, but shared facility language must be included when a customer asks about cross-contact risk.

Products that intentionally contain a major allergen must list it in the product catalog and on the product label. If a customer says they have a severe allergy, support should not reassure them that a product is safe. The correct response is to share the label information and recommend consulting a qualified professional before use.

## Product-Specific Allergen Notes

Magnesium Glycinate Calm does not intentionally contain milk, soy, gluten, peanuts, tree nuts, fish, shellfish, or sesame. It is produced in the shared facility described in this policy. The capsule is plant-based and the product is not flavored.

Vanilla Plant Protein contains pea protein, rice protein, natural vanilla flavor, and sunflower lecithin. It does not intentionally contain milk or soy, but it is made in the shared facility. Daily Greens Citrus contains wheatgrass and barley grass harvested before grain formation, but customers with gluten concerns should be directed to the label and a qualified professional.

## Customer-Facing Guidance

Support agents may say that allergen information appears on each product page and label. They may explain whether a product intentionally contains a listed allergen when that fact is present in the product catalog.

Support agents must not say a product is allergy-safe, hypoallergenic, or guaranteed free from cross-contact. If a customer reports symptoms after using a product, support should follow the support escalation SOP and avoid medical interpretation.

## Internal Handling

If a product page and this policy conflict, support should escalate to Quality Review before answering. If a customer asks for a certificate, lab report, or manufacturing record, support may acknowledge the request but must escalate because those documents are not available to frontline support.
