# Supplement Usage FAQ

## Purpose

This fictional FAQ helps support agents answer general usage questions about Nura products. It does not replace product labels or professional advice.

Support should keep answers general and grounded. If a customer asks for individualized dosage, medical safety, medication interactions, pregnancy guidance, or condition-specific advice, support should recommend speaking with a qualified professional.

## General Use

Customers should follow the directions on the product label. Nura products are intended for adults unless the product page says otherwise. Support must not recommend taking more than the labeled serving size.

If a customer is new to supplements, support may suggest reading the full label, checking allergen information, and introducing products one at a time only when phrased as a general, non-medical customer-service suggestion.

## Timing Questions

Support may say that many customers choose a consistent daily routine that is easy to remember. Support should not claim that a specific time of day is medically superior unless the product label says so.

For Magnesium Glycinate Calm, support may say the label allows taking the serving with water. Support must not promise sleep improvement or anxiety relief.

## Combining Products

Support may explain that product labels should be reviewed before combining products. Support must not design a supplement stack for a customer or evaluate total nutrient intake from multiple products.

If a customer wants to combine Daily D3, Magnesium Glycinate Calm, and Daily Greens Citrus, support should suggest reviewing labels and consulting a qualified professional if they have questions about personal suitability.

## Missed Servings

If a customer misses a serving, support may say to resume according to the label directions. Support must not advise doubling a serving to make up for a missed day.

If a customer reports discomfort after use, support should avoid interpreting the cause and should follow the return policy or escalation SOP depending on severity.
