# Discount and Refund Approval Rules

## Purpose

This fictional policy defines which goodwill discounts and refund exceptions support agents can offer without manager approval. It keeps customer treatment consistent while protecting the company from unsupported promises.

These rules apply to retail customers only. Wholesale, influencer, affiliate, and marketplace orders use separate agreements and must be escalated when the customer requests a refund exception.

## Agent Approval Limits

Support agents may offer a one-time 10 percent goodwill discount when a customer experienced a minor inconvenience such as a delayed response, damaged outer packaging, or confusion caused by unclear site copy. The discount must be applied to a future order and cannot be exchanged for cash.

Support agents may approve standard refunds that match the return policy. They may not approve refunds for final-sale bundles, expired return windows, wholesale orders, or repeated goodwill requests without manager review.

## Manager Approval Required

Manager approval is required for refund exceptions above 50 dollars, refund requests after the standard return window, opened product exceptions outside the return policy, and any customer who has received two goodwill credits in the last 180 days.

If a customer threatens a chargeback, legal action, public complaint, or regulator complaint, support should not negotiate a special refund in chat. The case must be escalated to a support manager with the full order timeline.

## Discount Restrictions

Discounts cannot be stacked with subscription renewal discounts, wholesale pricing, influencer codes, or replacement shipments. A customer may use only one active promotional code per order unless a manager creates a manual exception.

Support must not describe a discount as compensation for illness, injury, or medical harm. If the customer reports a health concern, the case must follow the health and safety escalation process first.

## Documentation

Every exception must include an internal note with the order number, reason, amount, agent name, and approval path. If the exception relates to damage or shipping, support should attach photos when the customer provides them.
