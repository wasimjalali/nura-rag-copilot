# Ingredient Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary gives support agents plain-language descriptions of fictional ingredients used in Nura Wellness products. It is not a medical reference and must not be used to diagnose conditions or recommend treatment.

Support agents may use this glossary to explain what an ingredient is and why it appears in a product. They should not make safety judgments for pregnant customers, children, customers with diagnosed conditions, or customers taking medication.

## Magnesium Glycinate

Magnesium glycinate is a form of magnesium paired with glycine. In Nura product copy, it may be described as supporting normal muscle function and relaxation. The approved description does not include claims about treating anxiety, insomnia, migraines, or digestive disorders.

Magnesium Glycinate Calm contains 200 mg of magnesium per serving. Customers should follow the product label unless a qualified professional gives different guidance.

## Vitamin D3

Vitamin D3 is included in Nura Daily D3 Softgels. Approved language says it supports normal immune function and bone health. Support must not say that Vitamin D3 prevents infections, treats deficiency, or replaces medical testing.

Daily D3 Softgels use a vegetarian softgel in this fictional product catalog. Customers with pregnancy, medication, or medical-condition questions should be directed to a qualified professional.

## Pea And Rice Protein

Vanilla Plant Protein uses a blend of pea protein and rice protein. The product is designed as a convenient protein source for adults. Support may explain that the blend is plant-based and does not intentionally contain milk or soy.

Support must not promise that the protein powder causes weight loss, muscle gain, or medical benefits. Customers asking about athletic or medical nutrition plans should consult a qualified professional.

## Botanical Blend

Daily Greens Citrus includes a fictional botanical blend with wheatgrass, barley grass, spirulina, chlorella, and citrus bioflavonoids. Approved language says it supports daily nutrient intake as part of a balanced diet.

Support should not describe the greens product as detoxifying the body, cleansing organs, curing fatigue, or replacing vegetables. If a customer asks about allergies or gluten concerns, support should refer to the allergen policy.
