# Support Escalation SOP

## Purpose

This fictional standard operating procedure tells support agents when to escalate customer conversations. Escalation protects customers, agents, and the company when a request requires specialized review.

Support agents should document what the customer said, what policy was used, and what action was taken. They should avoid adding medical opinions or legal interpretations to internal notes.

## Health And Safety Escalation

Escalate immediately if a customer reports severe symptoms such as trouble breathing, swelling of the face or throat, chest pain, fainting, emergency care, hospitalization, or a reaction involving a child. Support should advise the customer to seek urgent medical help if they believe it is an emergency.

For non-severe discomfort, support should express empathy, avoid medical interpretation, recommend consulting a qualified professional if concerned, and apply the return policy when applicable.

## Policy Exception Escalation

Escalate refund exceptions outside the standard return window, wholesale refund requests, repeated goodwill requests, chargeback threats, legal threats, regulator complaints, and requests for manufacturing records.

The escalation note should include order number, customer summary, requested outcome, relevant policy section, and any screenshots or photos provided by the customer.

## Quality Review Escalation

Escalate when a customer reports a possible contamination issue, incorrect label, missing seal, unusual odor, foreign material, or mismatch between the product page and received item.

Support may offer a replacement only when the shipping or refund policy allows it. Quality Review decides whether product investigation, lot tracking, or supplier follow-up is needed.

## Response Tone

Support should be calm, concise, and empathetic. Agents should not blame the customer, diagnose the issue, or overpromise outcomes. The goal is to route the case correctly and preserve a clear record.

When escalating, support should tell the customer that the case is being reviewed by the appropriate team and that follow-up timing depends on the issue type.
